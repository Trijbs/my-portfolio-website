The Practice

Design and code are the same gesture, just different tools.

I'm a designer and developer who builds from first principles. The portfolio spans interactive experiences, brand identities, mobile apps, full-stack builds, and automated systems.

The most interesting problems live between disciplines — where a visual decision is also a technical one, and the line between designer and engineer stops mattering.

I started with graphic design, moved into front-end, and kept expanding from there. Today I build interactive experiences with Three.js and WebGL, design visual systems in Figma, develop full-stack apps with React and Next.js, and build automated tools when something should exist and doesn't yet. I also run a separate creative universe at mvxworld.art — a personal identity hub I treat as a living archive and design experiment.

What drives me is originality. Work that couldn't belong to anyone else. Work that has a point of view before it needs an explanation.

01

First principles

I prefer building from scratch over assembling templates. Understanding the why makes every decision sharper.

02

Design close to code

Design decisions made without understanding the implementation rarely survive contact with reality. I close that gap by working both sides.

03

Originality over optimization

A portfolio should have a point of view. Work that could belong to anyone isn't worth making. The interesting stuff lives past the generic solution.
